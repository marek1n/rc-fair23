Social Intelligence - Seeing faces in context

The reverse correlation method is a data-driven method increasingly used in social psychology to study how people perceive faces. Click on questions below to learn more.

What influences human face perception?

Our perception is always affected by our expectations. When perceiving faces we take into account what we know about the person as well as numerous context clues.

What is the method?

A grayscale base image is overlayed with specific random noise filters, yielding a stimulus image and its exact inverse at each trial. In effect, a large feature space is sampled by this method without the need for a priori hypotheses. At the end of the experiment, the average of all selected images is computed (click on the button below to see your results).

How is it used?

We are trying to visualize the influence of semantic context on the perception of facial expressions. This idea is called knowledge-augemented face processing
